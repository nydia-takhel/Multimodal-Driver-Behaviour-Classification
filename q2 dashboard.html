Behaviour Labelling
Classification Thresholds
Rule-based labels derived from road safety literature. No human-annotated ground truth exists in the DLR-UT dataset.
🔴 AGGRESSIVE
accel > 2.0 m/s²
OR speed > 14.0 m/s (50 km/h)
OR yaw_change > 15°/step
Logic: ANY one condition triggers
Source: Bellem et al. (2016) for acceleration; StVO §3 for speed
🟢 SAFE
accel < 0.5 m/s²
AND speed < 8.0 m/s (29 km/h)
AND yaw_change < 5°/step
Logic: ALL three conditions must hold
Source: Conservative urban threshold
🟡 MODERATE
All observations not
classified as aggressive
or safe — default class
Logic: Catch-all

Methodology
End-to-End Pipeline
1
Data Loading & 4-Dataset Merge
All 96 CSV files per modality loaded. Four datasets merged via merge_asof with nearest-timestamp tolerances.
16,324,056 rows · 39 columns · 0 NaN
2
Rule-Based Behaviour Labelling
Physics-based thresholds applied per row. Aggressive 4.1% · Safe 47.2% · Moderate 48.7%.
Sources: Bellem et al. 2016 · StVO §3
3
Vehicle-Level Stratified Sampling
500 vehicles per dominant class (1,500 total). ALL rows retained per vehicle — trajectory continuity preserved. Row-level sampling would give ~9 rows/vehicle.
1,500 vehicles · 724,304 rows · avg 483 rows/vehicle
4
Vehicle-Level 80/20 Split Before Sequencing
1,200 vehicles train · 300 vehicles test. Split before sequence building — no vehicle appears in both sets. Prevents trajectory memorisation.
No vehicle leakage between train and test sets
5
Train-Only Feature Scaling
StandardScaler fitted on training vehicles only, then applied to all. Fitting on full dataset leaks test statistics into training.
scaler.fit(train_vehicles_only)
6
Sequence Building — 500ms Prediction Horizon
Sliding window of 30 timesteps (1.5s at 20Hz). Target label is 10 steps (500ms) ahead — not the immediate next step, which would trivially exploit autocorrelation.
473,353 train · 50,721 val · 140,325 test sequences
7
Validation-Monitored Training
10% of training vehicles reserved for validation. ReduceLROnPlateau and early stopping watch validation loss only. Test set touched exactly once after training.
Scheduler & early stopping on val loss — not train loss

Short-Term Classifier
Type: Vanilla Recurrent NN
Layers: 2 · Hidden: 64 · Dropout: 0.2
FC head: 64→32→3 (ReLU)

Lowest latency (0.07ms/batch) — best for real-time edge deployment. Competitive with LSTM on short 30-step sequences because vanishing gradients are minimal at this length. With proper validation monitoring, RNN generalised best of all three models.
LSTM
Medium-Term Classifier
Type: Long Short-Term Memory
Layers: 2 · Hidden: 64 · Dropout: 0.2
FC head: 64→32→3 (ReLU)
98.44%
ACCURACY · Macro F1: 97.21% · 0.10ms
Gating mechanism (forget/input/output gates) retains driving context across the full 1.5-second window. Early stopping triggered at epoch 24 — fastest to generalise. Best suited for detecting sustained aggressive behaviour patterns over multiple timesteps.
Transformer
Global Attention Classifier
Type: Encoder · 2 layers · 4 heads
d_model: 64 · FF dim: 128
Pos encoding: Learnable embedding
98.37%
ACCURACY · Macro F1: 97.17% · 0.35ms
Self-attention relates any two timesteps simultaneously. Highest computational cost (0.35ms). At 20Hz, consecutive timesteps are highly autocorrelated, limiting the advantage of global attention over recurrent memory at this sequence length. Strongest potential at longer horizons.

Verdict
Trajectory features alone achieve 98.85%. Adding all other modalities reduces accuracy by −0.53pp. For rule-defined kinematic labels, trajectory is the primary signal. Multi-modal fusion would benefit most from independently annotated labels.

Limitations
Limitations
Single Intersection · Single Day
Results are from one intersection in Braunschweig on one day. Generalisation to other intersections, cities, or traffic cultures is not validated.
Rule-Based Labels — No Ground Truth
Labels were constructed from kinematic thresholds, not human annotation. The model reproduces the rules with 98%+ accuracy — not necessarily real-world behaviour.
Contextual Features Do Not Help
Ablation confirmed weather, AQ, and signal features reduce performance by 0.53pp. For independently annotated labels, contextual features would likely contribute more.
Transformer at Short Sequences
Global self-attention adds limited benefit at 30-step windows with high autocorrelation at 20Hz. Its structural advantage increases at longer prediction horizons.
Future Scope
Next Steps
Human-Annotated Labels
Expert annotation against dashcam footage would enable genuine behaviour classification independent of kinematic rules — unlocking the full value of multi-modal features.
Multi-Intersection Datasets
Cross-site generalisation testing across multiple intersections and cities would validate whether learned patterns transfer across traffic environments.
Longer Prediction Horizons
Extending from 500ms to 2–5 seconds makes the task harder and more valuable. Transformer's attention mechanism would offer greater structural advantage.
Graph Neural Networks
Modelling vehicle-to-vehicle interactions at intersections captures social driving dynamics not possible with per-vehicle sequences.
Uncertainty Quantification
MC Dropout or conformal prediction would provide confidence intervals alongside classifications — essential for safety-critical deployment.
Cross-Environment Adaptation
The methodology is applicable to any intersection with multi-sensor trajectory infrastructure, given locally calibrated thresholds and sensor types.
